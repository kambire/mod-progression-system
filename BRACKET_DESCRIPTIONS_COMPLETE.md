# BRACKET DESCRIPTIONS - Complete Reference Guide

## VANILLA (Levels 1-60)

### Bracket_0
**Level Range**: 1-10  
**Content**: Pre-Launch / Early Game Introduction  
**Description**: Starting zones with basic dungeons and world content introduction. Includes Shadowfang Keep (Silverpine), Deadmines (Elwynn), Wailing Caverns (Mulgore), Blackfathom Deeps (Ashenvale), and Ragefire Chasm (Orgrimmar).  
**Highlights**: First dungeons for new players, class-specific quests, basic reputation farming  
**Historical Release**: November 23, 2004

---

### Bracket_1_19
**Level Range**: 10-19  
**Content**: Early Dungeons & World Content  
**Description**: Mid-tier introductory dungeons and world content progression. Includes Shadowfang Keep, Deadmines, Wailing Caverns, Blackfathom Deeps, and Ragefire Chasm with deeper progression.  
**Highlights**: Class dungeons unlocking, faction reputation increases, crafting recipes, world boss introduction (Azuregos zone access)  
**Historical Release**: November 23, 2004

---

### Bracket_20_29
**Level Range**: 20-29  
**Content**: Mid-Level Dungeons  
**Description**: Expansion of dungeon content with increased difficulty. Features Stormwind Stockades (Human-specific), Razorfen Kraul (Tauren starting area), and Gnomeregan (Dwarf/Gnome area).  
**Highlights**: Reputation farming begins seriously, professions unlock, PvP intro (World PvP enabled)  
**Historical Release**: November 23, 2004

---

### Bracket_30_39
**Level Range**: 30-39  
**Content**: Advanced Dungeons & World Content  
**Description**: More challenging dungeons with higher-level enemies and better loot. Includes Uldaman (Dwarf heritage), Razorfen Downs (undead zone), and Scarlet Monastery (Scarlet Crusade stronghold).  
**Highlights**: Level 30 water form unlocks, profession recipes increase, world bosses become more relevant  
**Historical Release**: November 23, 2004

---

### Bracket_40_49
**Level Range**: 40-49  
**Content**: World Dungeons & Raid Attunement Preparation  
**Description**: Challenging world dungeons and preparation for raid content. Features Dire Maul (36-48), Blackrock Depths (54-60 accessible), Scholomance (60), and Stratholme (60). World Bosses: Dragons of Nightmare (Duskwood, Feralas, Ashenvale), Lord Kazzak (Tainted Scar).  
**Highlights**: Attunement quest chains begin, world boss farming, pre-raid gear acquisition, PvP intensifies  
**Historical Release**: November 23, 2004

---

### Bracket_50_59_1
**Level Range**: 50-59  
**Content**: Upper Blackrock Spire (UBRS) Attunement Phase  
**Description**: Attunement-required instance. Players must complete a lengthy quest chain to enter UBRS and prepare for raid progression. Contains world bosses for top-tier gear before raids.  
**Highlights**: Attunement quest chain progression, first raid-level difficulty encounters, epic world boss battles (Azuregos, Dragons, Kazzak), pre-raid gear farming  
**Historical Release**: November 23, 2004

---

### Bracket_50_59_2
**Level Range**: 50-59  
**Content**: Upper Blackrock Spire (UBRS) Progression  
**Description**: Full access to UBRS with all bosses and encounters available. Players with attunement can progress through this 10-man raid-like instance.  
**Highlights**: UBRS boss farming for gear, world bosses, dungeon gear optimization, final preparation for Molten Core  
**Historical Release**: November 23, 2004

---

### Bracket_60_1_1
**Level Range**: 60  
**Content**: Molten Core (Raid Tier 1) & Era Appropriate Content  
**Description**: First true raid instance released, Molten Core is a 40-man raid located inside Blackrock Mountain. Players gather to defeat Lucifron, Magmadar, Gehennas, Garr, Shazzrah, Baron Geddon, Golemagg the Incinerator, and Ragnaros.  
**Raid Bosses**: 8 bosses (Lucifron, Magmadar, Gehennas, Garr, Shazzrah, Baron Geddon, Golemagg, Ragnaros)  
**Release Date**: November 23, 2004  
**Highlights**: First experience with 40-man coordination, epic world-class encounters, highest damage output gear at the time, world-changing boss mounts  
**Historical Release**: November 23, 2004

---

### Bracket_60_1_2
**Level Range**: 60  
**Content**: Onyxia's Lair (Raid Tier 1 Optional)  
**Description**: Separate raid instance featuring the infamous dragon Onyxia in her lair. 40-man raid with a single boss encounter but extremely challenging and rewarding.  
**Raid Bosses**: 1 boss (Onyxia)  
**Release Date**: November 23, 2004 (with Molten Core launch)  
**Highlights**: Iconic dragon encounter, first real DPS check, class-specific debuff challenge (Onyxia scales player resistances), exclusive dragon mount loot, world PvP around Blackrock Mountain  
**Historical Release**: November 23, 2004

---

### Bracket_60_2_1
**Level Range**: 60  
**Content**: Blackwing Lair (Raid Tier 2)  
**Description**: Advanced 40-man raid instance set in the blackflights' stronghold. Expanded raid progression with multiple dragons and encounters, introducing significant mechanic complexity.  
**Raid Bosses**: 8 bosses (Razorgore, Vaelastrasz, Broodlord, Firemaw, Ebonroc, Taerar, Ysondre, Lord Victor Nefarius)  
**Release Date**: January 19, 2005 (~2 months after launch)  
**Highlights**: Fire resistance gear requirements, dragon encounters, world PvP buff control (Blackrock dragons), world-class mechanics, Blackwing Dragon mounts  
**Historical Release**: January 19, 2005

---

### Bracket_60_2_2
**Level Range**: 60  
**Content**: Zul'Gurub (Raid Tier 2.5 / Optional)  
**Description**: 20-man raid instance in the jungles of Stranglethorn Vale. Smaller raid size than MC/BWL but more challenging mechanics. Features troll leadership and voodoo magic themes.  
**Raid Bosses**: 6 bosses (High Priest Thekal, High Priestess Mar'li, High Priest Venoxis, Bloodlord Mandokir, Edge of Madness, Gahz'ranka, Hakkar)  
**Release Date**: January 19, 2005  
**Highlights**: Voodoo/troll aesthetic, unique 20-man format, world bosses outside (dragons), reputation farming for jungle-themed items, PvP-heavy zone  
**Historical Release**: January 19, 2005

---

### Bracket_60_3_1
**Level Range**: 60  
**Content**: Ruins of Ahn'Qiraj (AQ20) - 20-man Raid  
**Description**: 20-man raid instance in the Silithus desert, featuring the insectoid Qiraji empire. Focus on bug-type encounters with sandstorm mechanics.  
**Raid Bosses**: 3 bosses (Kurinnaxx, General Rajaxx, Ossirian the Unscarred) + optional encounter (Taerar)  
**Release Date**: January 19, 2005  
**Highlights**: Silithus zone introduction, insect encounters, reputation farming (Cenarion Circle), unique quest chains  
**Historical Release**: January 19, 2005

---

### Bracket_60_3_2
**Level Range**: 60  
**Content**: Temple of Ahn'Qiraj (AQ40) - 40-man Raid Tier 3  
**Description**: Large 40-man raid instance representing the ultimate confrontation with the Qiraji bug empire. Multiple bosses including various insect types and the legendary Bug God C'Thun.  
**Raid Bosses**: 9 bosses (The Four Dragons of Nightmare, Taerar, Mstruthas, Vem, Yauj, Taur, Fankriss, Viscidus, Princess Huhuran, Twin Emperors Vek'lor & Vek'nilash, C'Thun)  
**Release Date**: January 19, 2005  
**Highlights**: Bug-themed encounters, nature vs. insect storyline, C'Thun mechanic complexity, silithid farming, Cenarion faction dominance  
**Historical Release**: January 19, 2005

---

### Bracket_60_3_3
**Level Range**: 60  
**Content**: AQ Post-Content Adjustments (Vanilla Final Phase)  
**Description**: Adjustments and world events following the opening of the Ahn'Qiraj gates. Dragons of Nightmare activated, world quest hubs established, high-end dungeons made more accessible.  
**Content**: Dragons of Nightmare (Taerar, Lethendris, Letheras, Taresadun), Silithus world events, reputation farming peaks  
**Highlights**: World event coordination, dragon hunting in open world, final Vanilla phase preparation  
**Historical Release**: January 19, 2005 onwards

### Bracket_Custom
**Level Range**: All Levels  
**Content**: Custom User-Defined Content  
**Description**: Reserved for custom scripts, SQL files, and modifications added by server administrators. Files in this bracket are loaded LAST, after all other brackets have been processed.  
**Usage**: Custom events, private scripts, house rules, custom mechanics  
**Important Notes**:
- Custom SQL files should be named with `progression_custom_%%%%` convention
- Custom scripts should follow naming and loader conventions similar to other brackets
- Files are loaded AFTER all other brackets
- Useful for server-specific modifications without affecting core progression
